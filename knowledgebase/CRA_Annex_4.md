# Annex IV – Critical Products with Digital Elements

> **Regulation (EU) 2024/2847 – adopted 23 October 2024, in force 10 December 2024**

> **New annex — no direct equivalent in the 2022 proposal.** Critical products were previously listed as "Class II" under Annex III. They are now separated into a standalone annex reflecting their higher risk profile and stricter conformity requirements.

> **Conformity assessment implications:** Manufacturers of critical products must either obtain a European cybersecurity certificate under an applicable European cybersecurity certification scheme (where mandated by delegated act under Article 8(1)), or, absent such a scheme, undergo mandatory third-party conformity assessment (Module B+C or Module H). Self-assessment (Module A) is not permitted. Technical descriptions of all categories are provided in **Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2025/2392** (published 28 November 2025).

---

The following categories of products with digital elements are considered critical:

(1) Hardware devices with security boxes;

(2) Smart meter gateways within smart metering systems as defined in Article 2, point (23), of Directive (EU) 2019/944;

(3) Smartcards or similar devices, including secure elements, used in the context of high-trust applications (e.g. electronic identification, digital signatures, or payment);

(4) Hardware Security Modules (HSMs) used in contexts critical to national infrastructure or essential services;

(5) Secure cryptoprocessors intended for use in critical infrastructure or for national security-adjacent applications.

> **Note:** The Commission may, by delegated act under Article 8, add or remove categories of critical products. Such acts must be preceded by an impact assessment and consultation with relevant stakeholders including the European Cybersecurity Certification Group. Products added via delegated act may be subject to mandatory European cybersecurity certification where a relevant scheme is already in place.
