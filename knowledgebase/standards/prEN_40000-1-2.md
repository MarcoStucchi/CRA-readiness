## 1. Purpose and scope

This standard specifies:
- Generic cybersecurity **principles** applicable to the full product lifecycle (Clause 5 — informative)
- Baseline requirements for **cybersecurity risk management** (Clause 6 — normative)
- Requirements for **cybersecurity lifecycle activities** during design, development, production, maintenance, and decommissioning (Clause 7 — normative)
- Framework elements for **vertical standards** development (Annex A — informative)

**Target users**: Manufacturers of all products with digital elements (hardware, software, Remote Data Processing Solutions/RDPS).

**CRA conformity role**: The combination of EN 40000-1-2 + EN 40000-1-3 + EN 40000-1-4 can serve as the basis for self-declaration under CRA Annex VIII Part I (Module A) for **Default class** products where no vertical standard is available.

**Scope limitation**: This document does NOT provide specific activities and requirements for vertical standards — those are provided by EN 304 6xx product-specific standards.

**RDPS note**: All references to "product" include Remote Data Processing Solutions where applicable. Any explicit reference to RDPS is solely to prompt consideration of RDPS-specific aspects.

## 4. Clause 5 — Cybersecurity principles (informative)

Four principles are established. These are foundational; Clauses 6 and 7 operationalise them.

### 5.1 Risk-based approach

**Principle**: Appropriate controls are implemented to address cybersecurity risks to persons, organisations, and society, based on identified risks throughout the lifecycle.

**Key concepts**:
- Risk characterised by **impact** (magnitude of loss/disruption) × **likelihood** (of occurrence)
- Controls address risk by altering impact, likelihood, or both
- Iterative process: context → acceptance criteria → assessment → treatment → communication → review

### 5.2 Security by design

**Principle**: Cybersecurity is integrated from concept phase throughout the entire lifecycle — not added after the fact.

**Techniques listed** (illustrative, not exhaustive):
- **Least privilege** — access limited to minimum necessary
- **Attack surface minimisation** — remove unnecessary interfaces
- **Defence in depth** — multiple layered controls
- **Secure coding practices** — language-specific rules, tools for defect detection
- **No security by obscurity** — rely on established mechanisms, not secret designs
- **User-centric design** — consider user needs and behaviours; minimise friction to avoid circumvention incentives
- **Lifecycle management** — controls covering design through decommissioning; secure by default at market placement; security updates during support period; secure decommissioning option

### 5.3 Secure by default

**Principle**: Default configuration is secure for intended purpose and reasonably foreseeable use (IPRFU) at time of market placement.

**Best practices** (apply risk-based, not all applicable to every product):
- Secure default configurations for all security-relevant settings
- Security features enabled by default
- Non-essential network services/interfaces disabled on first activation
- Product updated to most recent secure version on first install
- Remote administration disabled by default
- Telemetry/analytics: opt-in for consumers; contractual opt-in for business users
- Easy "Reset to secure defaults" action
- Explicit user action required to deviate from secure defaults; user warned of deviations
- Update mechanisms allowing recovery from partial/failed installations (rollback to secure state)
- Anti-rollback mechanisms to prevent installation of older vulnerable versions
- Security-relevant configuration options clearly documented

**Exceptions**:
- B2B tailor-made products: opt-out from secure by default possible with verifiable user agreement
- Single-use or custom products: reset to default may not be technically possible
- In all exception cases: users must be given appropriate risk information

### 5.4 Transparency

**Principle**: Adequate and relevant cybersecurity information is made available to relevant interested parties (users, integrators, supply-chain actors) in accessible and useable formats.

**Information covered** (not exhaustive):
- Information and instructions to the user
- Technical documentation content
- Update availability and risks of delayed updates
- Current security state of the product
- Relevant risks, risk status, and potential mitigations
- Incident information

**Note**: Different interested parties require different information — integrators need more technical depth than end users. Communication should consider accessibility (Annex C).

## 5. Clause 6 — Product cybersecurity risk management activities

Risk management applies to ALL phases of the lifecycle. It is iterative: changes in product design, threat landscape, or context trigger repetition of relevant activities. Six activities, each with normative requirements and assessment criteria.

### 6.1 Overview — Risk management activity sequence

1. **6.2** Establish product context
2. **6.3** Establish risk acceptance criteria
3. **6.4** Conduct risk assessment (asset identification → threat identification → risk analysis → risk evaluation)
4. **6.5** Risk treatment
5. **6.6** Risk communication
6. **6.7** Risk review

---

### 6.2 Product context

**Purpose**: Establish the starting point for risk assessment. Iterative — changes to product or context require review.

**Context elements**:

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| IPRFU | Intended purpose and reasonably foreseeable use — defines scope for risk identification |
| Product functions | Functions specified by manufacturer including cybersecurity-protective functions and configuration capability |
| Operational environment | Networks, systems, physical surroundings, assistive technology compatibility, people/animals external to product; access levels: public / limited / controlled |
| Architecture overview | Key components, interfaces, dependencies — high-level; includes RDPS where applicable |
| User description | Classification: **vulnerable** (children, disabilities, AT users), **ordinary** (no security skills), **instructed** (trained/supervised), **skilled** (specialist knowledge) |
| Distribution of security functions | What security functions the product provides for itself, requires from its environment, and provides to other products |

**Requirement [RMA-01-RQ-01]**: Product context SHALL be identified and documented including: IPRFU, functions, operational environment, architecture overview, user description, distribution of security functions (if available).

**Requirement [RMA-01-RQ-02]**: If product has RDPS, it SHALL be considered in the product context.

**Output**: Documentation of product context per RMA-01-RQ-01.

**Assessment criteria**: Check documentation exists. Assess whether it meets requirements and is appropriate for the product. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

---

### 6.3 Risk acceptance criteria

**Purpose**: Define the threshold for determining whether risk treatment is needed and whether applied treatment is sufficient.

**Factors informing risk acceptance criteria** (at least):
- Relevant regulatory factors
- Supply chain considerations (contractual agreements, downstream integrators, reasonable expectations)
- Nature of known risks (health/safety, severity/likelihood, inherent/unavoidable risks)
- State of the art

**Additional factors**:
- Estimated number of products on market — aggregated risk may be unacceptable even if single-product risk seems acceptable
- User capabilities to mitigate risk further
- Operational environment and RDPS hosting platform

**Risk sharing**: Risks may be acceptable when shared with users or downstream integrators, provided conditions of use are clear and communicated. Risk sharing does NOT release manufacturer of legal obligations.

**Exceptions**: Deviations from criteria are justified when user explicitly accepts risk (e.g. tailor-made products) — communicate residual risk throughout supply chain.

**Requirement [RMA-02-RQ-01]**: Risk acceptance criteria SHALL be defined, justified for product context (6.2), and documented considering at least: regulatory factors, supply chain considerations, nature of known risks, state of the art.

**Note**: Vertical standards may provide risk acceptance criteria for specific product categories — using these is justified and aligned with state of the art.

**Output**: Product cybersecurity risk acceptance criteria per 6.3.3.

**Assessment criteria**: Check criteria exist. Assess whether criteria are based on user needs, reasonable and justified for product context, and meet requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

---

### 6.4 Product cybersecurity risk assessment

Four sequential sub-activities:

#### 6.4.1 Asset identification

**Purpose**: Determine what is to be protected.

**Asset categories**:
- **Product assets**: data (cryptographic keys, personal data, financial data, log data), functions (configuration, communication, cryptographic protocol integrity), software/hardware components and interfaces
- **Supporting assets**: software/hardware components and interfaces, update/patch processes, network and adjacent devices
- **User assets**: health and safety, data protection (manipulation/fraud/privacy), function availability, assistive technology compatibility
- **Environmental assets**: network and adjacent devices, health/safety of other persons or domestic animals, public interest

**Note on component products**: For components (microcontrollers, processors) whose end-product integration is not fully known, focus on protecting the functionality of the component itself within the IPRFU.

**Requirement [RMA-03-RQ-01]**: Product cybersecurity assets and their cybersecurity properties (CIA + others) SHALL be identified based on product context (6.2).

**Output**: List of product cybersecurity assets and their cybersecurity properties.

**Assessment criteria**: Check list exists. Assess applicability and representativeness for product context. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

#### 6.4.2 Threat identification

**Purpose**: Identify threats that could affect identified assets within the product context.

**Threat modelling approaches** (referenced, not mandated):
- Attack trees (Schneier)
- PASTA (Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis)
- STRIDE (OWASP)
- TARA (Threat Analysis and Risk Assessment)
- EN IEC 62443-4-1 / ETSI TS 102 165-1 (TVRA)

**Sources for threat identification**: TR 40000-1-5 threat catalogue, sector-specific catalogues (MITRE), product-specific ENs, known vulnerabilities from public databases.

**Requirement [RMA-04-RQ-01]**: Product cybersecurity threats SHALL be identified and include: targeted asset(s) including potentially compromised cybersecurity properties; cause of the potential compromise.

**Output**: List of product cybersecurity threats.

**Assessment criteria**: Check list exists. Assess justification for product context. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

#### 6.4.3 Risk analysis

**Purpose**: Estimate likelihood and impact for each identified threat.

**Estimation approaches**:
- Qualitative (risk matrices — likelihood category × impact category → risk category)
- Quantitative (scoring systems mapping qualitative categories to numerical values)
- Both likelihood and impact estimated from perspective of potentially affected users

**Likelihood factors**: discoverability, speed of materialisation, detectability by user.

**Impact factors**: health and safety, data loss, economic losses, impact on accessibility.

**Requirement [RMA-05-RQ-01]**: Likelihood of occurrence SHALL be estimated for each identified threat.

**Requirement [RMA-05-RQ-02]**: Impact of potential cybersecurity incidents SHALL be estimated for each identified threat.

**Requirement [RMA-05-RQ-03]**: Scenario of potential cybersecurity incidents in which the risk exists SHALL be documented.

**Output**: For each risk — likelihood, impact, risk scenario documented.

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess consistency of analysis. Assess output meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

#### 6.4.4 Risk evaluation and acceptance

**Purpose**: Compare identified risks against risk acceptance criteria; justify acceptable residual risks.

**Risk acceptance**: Conscious decision to acknowledge risk without further action, provided it aligns with risk acceptance criteria (6.3). Accepted residual risk is shared with users — conditional on clear communication.

**Important notes**:
- Risk sharing is especially relevant for component products
- If risk cannot be addressed, manufacturer may need to organise a product recall
- Mitigation shared to user must be within the capability of the intended user type

**Requirement [RMA-06-RQ-01]**: Each risk SHALL be evaluated against risk acceptance criteria to determine if acceptable or requiring treatment.

**Requirement [RMA-06-RQ-02]**: Risks SHALL only be accepted when acceptable per risk acceptance criteria.

**Requirement [RMA-06-RQ-03]**: Where mitigation requires user action or specific operational environment, risks SHALL only be accepted if recommended measures are within capability of intended users.

**Requirement [RMA-06-RQ-04]**: Accepted risks SHALL be justified.

**Output**: Evaluation of each risk and justification for residual risk acceptance.

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists covering all identified risks. Assess justification aligned with risk acceptance criteria. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

---

### 6.5 Product cybersecurity risk treatment

**Purpose**: Address unacceptable risks until residual risk is acceptable.

**Treatment approaches**:
- **Risk avoidance**: remove source (e.g. remove unnecessary interfaces, avoid immature libraries, avoid legacy protocols, avoid external plugins/scripting unless necessary)
- **Risk mitigation**: implement controls to change likelihood or impact
- **Context alteration**: change intended purpose or conditions of use to adjust risk acceptance criteria
- **Risk sharing**: transfer to users or downstream integrators — requires communication

**Note**: Adding controls modifies the product and typically triggers a repeat of the risk assessment.

**Requirement [RMA-07-RQ-01]**: Each unacceptable risk SHALL be treated as appropriate to product context.

**Requirement [RMA-07-RQ-02]**: Risk treatment decision and rationale SHALL be documented.

**Requirement [RMA-07-RQ-03]**: After treatment, risks SHALL be re-evaluated.

**Output**: Documentation of risk treatment decision with justification per risk.

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess justification aligned with risk acceptance criteria. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

---

### 6.6 Product cybersecurity risk communication

**Purpose**: Communicate relevant residual risks to users, including required user actions.

**Accessibility consideration**: Communication must consider user capabilities including impairments — simple language, multi-modal formats.

**Requirement [RMA-08-RQ-01]**: Risk communication SHALL use methods and terminology meeting user communication needs; consumer information SHALL be in clear, non-technical language where possible.

**Requirement [RMA-08-RQ-02]**: Risk communication SHALL consider accessibility aspects (simple language, multi-modal formats).

**Requirement [RMA-08-RQ-03]**: Communication SHALL include: circumstances/misuse leading to significant cybersecurity risks; recommended user mitigation measures; conditions of use for secure operation.

**Output**:
- Documentation of circumstances and misuse leading to significant risks
- Documentation of conditions of use and recommended mitigation measures communicated to user

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess output meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

---

### 6.7 Product cybersecurity risk review

**Purpose**: Maintain appropriate cybersecurity by reviewing and updating risk management in response to changes.

**Review triggers**:
- Planned regular intervals (frequency based on product context and risks)
- Product modification impacting cybersecurity
- Change in product context
- Change in risk acceptance criteria over lifecycle
- Change in risk exposure (including threat landscape changes)
- Known exploitable vulnerability or actively exploited vulnerability identified
- Severe incident occurs
- Component becomes obsolete with cybersecurity impact

**Requirement [RMA-09-RQ-01]**: Risk management activities SHALL be reviewed at planned intervals based on risk assessment and product context.

**Requirement [RMA-09-RQ-02]**: Review SHALL also be performed when [all triggers listed in 6.7.3].

**Requirement [RMA-09-RQ-03]**: If impacted, relevant risk assessment documentation of Clause 6 SHALL be updated.

**Output**:
- Documentary evidence that review has been done
- Updated Clause 6 documentation if applicable

**Assessment criteria**: Check updates of documentation exist if triggered. Assess updates cover changes needed due to trigger. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

## 6. Clause 7 — Product cybersecurity lifecycle activities

**Process-agnostic**: Activities can be executed concurrently or in various orders. Compatible with agile, waterfall, and other methodologies. Many activities are prerequisites for others; revisit as needed.

**Dependencies**: Changes in one activity imply changes in others. Rigor of activities depends on risks identified in Clause 6.

### 7.1 [CLA-01] Product cybersecurity planning

**Purpose**: Describe cybersecurity activities for a product throughout its lifecycle. Living document — starts blank, evolves as information becomes available.

**Requirement [CLA-01-RQ-01]**: Planning SHALL cover at least all applicable activities described in Clause 6 and 7.3–7.10.

**Requirement [CLA-01-RQ-02]**: For the complete lifecycle, activities in the plan SHALL: be maintained and reviewed regularly; have their execution tracked.

**Note**: Planning may reference organisation-wide policies for activities common to multiple products.

**Output**: Documentation of product cybersecurity planning per requirements.

**Assessment criteria**: Check documentation exists. Assess meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.2 [CLA-02] Product cybersecurity requirements

**Purpose**: Specify what needs to be implemented, derived from product context and risk assessment. Manage throughout lifecycle.

**Inputs** (normative):
- Product context (6.2)
- Risk assessment (6.4)
- Risk treatment decisions (6.5)
- Applicable regulatory requirements (including GDPR for data minimisation)

**Requirement [CLA-02-RQ-01]**: Cybersecurity requirements SHALL be specified based on product context, risk treatment decisions, and applicable regulatory requirements.

**Requirement [CLA-02-RQ-02]**: Requirements SHALL be reviewed and updated when any of the listed inputs change.

**Output**: Documented cybersecurity requirements.

**Assessment criteria**: Check documentation exists. Assess meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.3 [CLA-03] Cybersecurity architecture and design

**Purpose**: Design aligned with cybersecurity requirements and product context. Select appropriate controls.

**Key design considerations**:
- Trust boundaries between components/subsystems including RDPS — separation of privileges, containment
- Secure interface design — authentication, input validation, attack surface minimisation
- Secure-by-default settings of components
- Internal and external interfaces and how they are secured
- Data-flow/trust-boundary diagram (including RDPS relation)
- Function availability
- Ability to separate functional and security updates

**Control sources** (illustrative, others permitted):
- EN 40000-1-4 (normative security controls for CRA)
- Vertical standards under M/606
- Other technically appropriate sources

**Requirement [CLA-03-RQ-01]**: Cybersecurity architecture and design SHALL be specified, including selection of appropriate controls to fulfil cybersecurity requirements (7.3). [Note: consider principles in 5.2 security by design and 5.3 secure by default]

**Requirement [CLA-03-RQ-02]**: Architecture and design SHALL ensure cybersecurity is maintained during component integration, including third-party components and RDPS.

**Output**: Documented cybersecurity architecture and design.

**Assessment criteria**: Check documentation exists. Assess meets requirements. Assess consistency with cybersecurity requirements (7.3). PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.4 [CLA-04] Secure implementation

**Purpose**: Develop and maintain product securely per architecture and design; produce required documentation.

**Key implementation activities** (non-exhaustive):
- **SBOM maintenance**: list components including at least top-level dependencies incl. third-party; relevant parts supporting efficient vulnerability monitoring
- **Dependency management**: select secure libraries; keep updated per 7.10
- **Hardware-based security functions** where applicable
- **Secure coding standards**: language-specific rules; prohibit hard-coded credentials; proper error handling
- **Memory safety**: avoid buffer overflows, memory leaks; safe programming languages; memory management tools
- **Technical documentation** (CRA Annex VII) preparation and maintenance
- **Information and instructions to user** (CRA Annex II) preparation

**Cryptography note**: ENISA "Agreed Cryptographic Mechanisms (ACM)" considered baseline for appropriate technology for cryptographic key and certificate management. Consider cryptographic agility for Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) transition during product support period.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-01]**: Product SHALL be developed and maintained in a secure manner to prevent unauthorised changes through use of a secure development environment.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-02]**: ALL cybersecurity requirements (7.3) per the architecture and design (7.4) SHALL be implemented.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-03]**: List of components integrated in the product SHALL be identified and maintained.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-04]**: An SBOM appropriate for the product SHALL be created and maintained.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-05]**: Technical documentation SHALL be prepared.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-06]**: Information and instructions to the user SHALL be prepared.

**Requirement [CLA-04-RQ-07]**: If implementation deviates from architecture/design documentation, reviews of risk management activities (6.7) and impacted lifecycle activities SHALL be triggered.

**Output**:
- Documentation on how development/maintenance is protected from unauthorised changes
- Product with implemented architecture and design (hardware, software, RDPS)
- List of components integrated into product
- SBOM in commonly used machine-readable format, consistent with risks/design/scope
- Documentation of third-party component intended purpose adherence (where applicable)
- Technical documentation and information/instructions to user
- Updated risk management and lifecycle activities documentation (if applicable)

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess output meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.5 [CLA-05] Cybersecurity verification and validation

**Purpose**: Verify cybersecurity controls fulfil requirements; validate product is secure within its context. Recurring activity throughout lifecycle — not only at final development stage.

**V&V approaches** (apply as appropriate to risks, design, scope):

| Approach | Description |
|---|---|
| Cybersecurity analysis | Systematic evaluation of artefacts (architecture docs, user docs, code) — static/dynamic analysis, inspections, peer reviews |
| Vulnerability scanning | Identify/assess vulnerabilities using tools, query vulnerability databases, identify defect class indicators |
| Hardware cybersecurity testing | Side-channel countermeasures, fault injection resistance, debug interface verification |
| Functional testing | Conformance testing — correct implementation of controls |
| Unit testing | Secure behaviour of functional software units; supports regression testing |
| Integration testing | Interaction between components; identify cybersecurity gaps at integration |
| System testing | Overall product cybersecurity and resilience to attack scenarios |
| Fuzz testing | Unexpected/random/invalid inputs — detect crashes, memory leaks, unexpected behaviour |
| Stress testing | Resource usage under load — DoS vulnerabilities, memory leaks, inefficient resource handling |
| Penetration testing | Purposeful attacks with varying knowledge levels |

**Note**: Carry out V&V in an isolated environment where possible.

**Requirement [CLA-05-RQ-01]**: V&V SHALL verify cybersecurity controls fulfil cybersecurity requirements (7.3.4).

**Requirement [CLA-05-RQ-02]**: V&V SHALL validate product is secure within its context (6.2.4) per risk assessment (6.4).

**Requirement [CLA-05-RQ-03]**: V&V activities SHALL, where appropriate, include:
- Analyse that implementation artefacts (incl. integrated components) fulfil requirements
- Validate that implemented design handles errors including input data validation
- V&V of external interfaces and protocols within cybersecurity context
- Additional security testing to discover vulnerabilities — may include penetration testing
- Review cybersecurity technical documentation and user instructions for accuracy/completeness
- Verify risk treatments align with cybersecurity requirements

**Requirement [CLA-05-RQ-04]**: V&V SHALL be performed as recurring activities aligned with 7.8.

**Requirement [CLA-05-RQ-05]**: V&V SHALL confirm cybersecurity risks from integrated components have been addressed in the implemented design.

**Requirement [CLA-05-RQ-06]**: V&V SHALL identify the selected test methods used.

**Output**: Documentation of V&V results (e.g. test reports) including selected test methods.

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess meets requirements. Assess evidence that controls fulfil requirements and are aligned with product context. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.6 [CLA-06/07] Secure production and distribution

Two sub-tracks — both applicable as relevant:

#### 7.6.1 Digital software products

**Purpose**: Protect software products and updates during distribution (compile, build, package, distribute). Reduces risk of manipulation by external actors during distribution.

**Requirement [CLA-06-RQ-01]**: Production and distribution of digital software products SHALL ensure software products and updates are protected from manipulation — both in transit and in storage.

**Requirement [CLA-06-RQ-02]**: Activities SHALL, where appropriate, include:
- Authentication for accessing protected areas of online hosting platform
- Protection of integrity of distribution artefacts (transit and storage)
- Mechanisms ensuring authenticity of distribution artefacts

**Requirement [CLA-06-RQ-03]**: Information and instructions for user SHALL be protected during distribution, including communication channels.

**Requirement [CLA-06-RQ-04]**: Information and instructions for user SHALL be made available in an accessible manner.

**Output**:
- Software product distribution artefacts ready for secure market placement
- Documentation of authentication and authenticity/integrity protection measures (where appropriate)
- Documentation of how communication channel for user instructions is secured and accessible

#### 7.6.2 Physical products

**Purpose**: Protect tangible products (hardware component or physical media) during manufacturing. Reduces risk of production process manipulation.

**Key aspects**:
- Change management for process changes — verify cybersecurity impact
- Protection of manufacturing systems and processes
- Protection of data supporting secure manufacturing
- Tamper-resistance or tamper-evidence for packaged products

**Requirement [CLA-07-RQ-01]**: Risks from hardware product or physical media being manipulated during manufacturing SHALL be addressed.

**Requirement [CLA-07-RQ-02]**: Activities SHALL, where appropriate, include:
- Protection of confidentiality, authenticity, integrity of production work instructions
- Verification of expected product components and their firmware/software/data
- Protection of installed software, data, configurations (keys, identifiers, advisories) — including restricting programming/testing features from unauthorised use
- Protection and authentication management of product security features (e.g. PKI management)

**Output**: Evidence of activities undertaken per requirements.

### 7.7 [CLA-08] Product cybersecurity monitoring and issue management

**Purpose**: Identify, receive, and process cybersecurity-related issues (vulnerabilities and incidents) from internal and external sources throughout lifecycle. Key aspect of product maintenance.

**Monitoring sources** (non-exhaustive):
- Vulnerability databases
- End-of-support notices for integrated components
- Cybersecurity laboratories
- Similar products on market
- Market surveillance authorities
- Third-party vulnerability disclosures
- Internal testing and review activities

**Requirement [CLA-08-RQ-01]**: Manufacturer SHALL monitor for changes in product risk assessment — including incidents and vulnerabilities — at periodic intervals justifiable for the product context.

**Requirement [CLA-08-RQ-02]**: Risk assessment (Clause 6.4) SHALL be updated if needed.

**Requirement [CLA-08-RQ-03]**: Discovered vulnerabilities SHALL be addressed in line with Clauses 6.4 and 6.6.

**Requirement [CLA-08-RQ-04]**: Verified cybersecurity incidents SHALL be addressed without undue delay where appropriate to the risk assessment and cybersecurity context.

**Requirement [CLA-08-RQ-05]**: Relevant residual risk arising from an incident SHALL be communicated in alignment with Clause 6.6.

**Output**:
- Documentation of monitoring intervals and justification for their periodicity
- Evidence of monitoring activities undertaken
- Where incident/vulnerability identified: risk assessment result; evidence of treatment without undue delay

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess meets requirements. Assess treatment decision resolves root cause. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.8 [CLA-09] Planning for secure decommissioning

**Purpose**: Prepare for securely resetting or retiring a product (including RDPS); ensure user assets are not compromised when product changes user or is disposed of.

**Key activities**: Secure deletion of user data, settings, cryptographic keys; reset to secure default state; user communication about end-of-support implications.

**Requirement [CLA-09-RQ-01]**: Manufacturer SHALL prepare for secure decommissioning, accounting for at least:
- Cybersecurity considerations for decommissioning
- Confidential assets to be securely deleted and their logical locations
- Where third-party or manufacturer operates parts of product: appropriate actions for secure decommissioning
- Relevant information and instructions to user regarding decommissioning
- Method by which user is informed about decommissioning
- Activities to be undertaken by manufacturer to ensure secure decommissioning

**Requirement [CLA-09-RQ-02]**: Relevant user information and instructions SHALL contain, where applicable, at least:
- How to remove user data (e.g. Factory Reset / Secure Erase)
- How to export user data
- How to remove cybersecurity assets (security-relevant configuration data)
- How to remove product from its operational environment
- How to securely dispose of product to prevent data disclosure
- For automatically deleted data: information on what will be deleted and when

**Output**:
- Documentation of method for communicating decommissioning to user
- Documentation of manufacturer activities to prepare for secure decommissioning
- Where applicable: user information and instructions per CLA-09-RQ-02

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.

### 7.9 [CLA-10] Third-party component cybersecurity management

**Purpose**: Ensure cybersecurity risks from third-party components (commercial, free, open-source) are handled with due diligence throughout lifecycle.

**Due diligence activities** (at selection and prior to integration):

| Activity | Details |
|---|---|
| Intended purpose alignment | Verify IPRFU of component aligns with its use/integration within product |
| Compliance verification | Check for CRA CE marking and EU Declaration of Conformity (if available) |
| Vulnerability check | No known vulnerabilities relevant to the product in public databases (e.g. EUVD per NIS2 Art. 12(2)) |
| Patch availability | Verify availability of cybersecurity patches |
| Vulnerability disclosure | Verify disclosure and handling process exists |
| Risk assessment | Check for component risk assessment |
| Additional testing | Determine if additional cybersecurity testing is required |
| Technical verification | Binary analysis, source code scanning, rebuilding from source, code reviews |

**For FOSS components specifically**:
- Active maintenance (evidence of recent development/maintenance activity)
- Vulnerability disclosure process defined and publicly available
- Stewarded for commercial use by foundation or organisation acting as open-source software steward
- Indicators of security-by-design practices (documented secure development practices)
- Regular cybersecurity patches/security updates available

**Ongoing obligations**:
- Consider support period of components when selecting — anticipate risks from deprecated components and obsolete technologies
- Include component risk in product risk assessment (6.4)
- Secure incorporation per 7.3 (architecture) and 7.4 (implementation)
- Monitor for vulnerabilities throughout product lifecycle (7.7)
- Communicate newly found vulnerabilities to responsible third-party (supplier, service provider, open-source steward)
- Best practice: contribute upstream fixes to original FOSS project

**Requirement [CLA-10-RQ-01]**: Due diligence SHALL be performed for third-party components during selection and prior to integration.

**Requirement [CLA-10-RQ-02]**: Third-party components and their integration SHALL be included in risk assessment and treatment (Clause 6).

**Requirement [CLA-10-RQ-03]**: Third-party components SHALL be securely implemented and integrated per 7.4.

**Requirement [CLA-10-RQ-04]**: Third-party components and their integration SHALL be validated and verified per 7.5.

**Requirement [CLA-10-RQ-05]**: Third-party components SHALL be monitored over their lifecycle to identify and address relevant vulnerabilities per 7.7.

**Output**: Documented evidence that relevant activities in CLA-10-RQ-01 through CLA-10-RQ-05 have been performed.

**Assessment criteria**: Check output exists. Assess meets requirements. PASS if all criteria fulfilled.
