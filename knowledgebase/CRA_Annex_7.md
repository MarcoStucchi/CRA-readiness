# Annex VII – Contents of the Technical Documentation

> **Regulation (EU) 2024/2847 – adopted 23 October 2024, in force 10 December 2024**

> **Changes from the 2022 proposal:** Renumbered from Annex V. Content significantly expanded: the cybersecurity risk assessment is now an explicit mandatory element; the software bill of materials (SBOM) must appear in the technical file; the support period determination and its rationale must be documented; architectural decisions about the software development and build process must be captured.

> **Retention period:** Technical documentation must be kept at the disposal of market surveillance authorities for at least 10 years after the product has been placed on the market, or for the duration of the support period, whichever is longer.

---

The technical documentation referred to in Article 31 shall contain at least the following information, as applicable to the relevant product with digital elements:

(1) a general description of the product with digital elements, including:

(a) its intended purpose and the security environment intended by the manufacturer;

(b) versions of software affecting compliance with essential cybersecurity requirements;

(c) where the product with digital elements is a hardware product, photographs or illustrations showing external features, marking and internal layout;

(d) user information and instructions as set out in Annex II;

(2) a description of the design, development and production of the product with digital elements and of the vulnerability handling processes, including:

(a) complete information on the design and development of the product, including, where applicable, drawings, schemes and/or a description of the system architecture, explaining how software components build on or feed into each other and integrate into the overall processing; this shall include architectural decisions relevant to the development and build process;

(b) complete information and specifications of the vulnerability handling processes put in place by the manufacturer, including:

- the software bill of materials (SBOM) in a commonly used and machine-readable format, covering at least the top-level dependencies;
- the coordinated vulnerability disclosure (CVD) policy;
- evidence of the provision of a contact address for reporting vulnerabilities;
- a description of the technical solutions chosen for the secure distribution of updates;

(c) complete information and specifications of the production and monitoring processes of the product with digital elements and their validation;

(3) the cybersecurity risk assessment carried out pursuant to Article 13(3), including:

(a) the risks considered;

(b) the essential cybersecurity requirements set out in Part I, point (2) of Annex I that are applicable and how they have been implemented, as informed by the risk assessment;

(c) the essential cybersecurity requirements set out in Part I, point (2) of Annex I that are not applicable, with a clear justification;

(d) how the manufacturer applies Part I, point (1) of Annex I and the vulnerability handling requirements in Part II of Annex I;

(4) the rationale for the determination of the support period of the product, including the information taken into account pursuant to Article 13(8);

(5) a list of the harmonised standards applied in full or in part the references of which have been published in the Official Journal of the European Union, common specifications as set out in Article 27(2), or European cybersecurity certification schemes under Regulation (EU) 2019/881 pursuant to Article 27(3); where those harmonised standards, common specifications or cybersecurity certification schemes have not been applied, descriptions of the solutions adopted to meet the essential requirements set out in Annex I, including a list of other relevant technical specifications applied; in the event of partly applied harmonised standards, common specifications or cybersecurity certifications, the technical documentation shall specify the parts which have been applied;

(6) reports of the tests carried out to verify the conformity of the product with digital elements and of the vulnerability handling processes with the applicable essential cybersecurity requirements set out in Annex I;

(7) a copy of the EU declaration of conformity;

(8) where applicable, the software bill of materials, further to a reasoned request from a market surveillance authority provided that it is necessary for that authority to be able to check compliance with the essential requirements set out in Annex I.
